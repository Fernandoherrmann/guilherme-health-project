My Biggest Realization

Before this project, I thought health was mostly about the body — exercise, food, and staying fit. But as I researched more, I realized health is much more than that.

It includes how we think, how we feel, and how we react to everyday situations.

🌍 WHO: "Health is not just the absence of disease, but a complete state of physical, mental, and social well-being."

🎯 My Goal

💡 Key Insight

The 3 Pillars

Three Types of Health

Physical, mental, and emotional — all equally important

💪

Physical Health

How well your body works and how you take care of it. When we take care of our bodies, we feel more energetic, stronger, and focused.

  • Eat nutritious foods
  • Exercise regularly
  • Get 8–10 hrs of sleep
  • Avoid too much fast food
  • Stay hydrated
🧠

Mental Health

How we think, handle stress, and make decisions. Mental health exists on a spectrum — it changes with life experiences and environment.

  • Cope with stress
  • Learn and work effectively
  • Realize your abilities
  • Contribute to society
  • Make good decisions
💛

Emotional Health

How we understand and manage our emotions. It affects how we react to situations, how we communicate, and how we build relationships.

  • Recognize feelings
  • Stay calm in difficulty
  • Build healthy relationships
  • Recover from challenges
  • Express emotions well

💡 "It's okay to feel emotions — what matters is how we handle them."

Interview with a Psychologist

Dra. Leila Dilmann

Clinical Psychologist — specializing in children and adolescents

🕐 18 years of experience
Q1

How can an unhealthy lifestyle affect your day-to-day life?

Mental illness can come from many different situations and factors. Physical health plays a fundamental role in emotional health — physical activity helps create balance so a person can have emotional stability. Body and mind work together. When the body is healthy, the child, teenager, and adult all benefit greatly. And the opposite is also true: a struggling body can compromise emotional health too.

💡 "Body and mind function together — when one is healthy, the other benefits too."
Q2

How can poor mental health harm the brain?

A child is always growing and the brain is developing along with them. When there are fragile psychological factors or emotional suffering, this makes various aspects of development more difficult: daily behavior, attitudes, physical health, cognitive function, and school performance.

💡 "Our mental health influences our entire healthy development — it affects every part of who we are."
Q3

What is usually the best treatment for mental health?

The first sessions are to identify symptoms — what is the suffering, what is the demand. From there, I evaluate whether psychological treatment alone will be enough, or whether other specialists are needed: a psychiatrist when medication is required, a neurologist, a nutritionist if there are eating difficulties, or a psycho-pedagogical specialist for learning challenges. The goal is always to build a team around the patient to bring stability and quality of life.

💡 "There is no one-size-fits-all treatment — each person needs an individualized path to health."
Q4

How does physical health affect mental health?

Physical health directly affects self-esteem and self-perception — how you feel about yourself and how you see yourself in relation to others. We are made of many parts — like pillars. Physical health, emotional health, and how we perceive ourselves are all pillars that need to be strong. Feeling good physically helps enormously in the psychological process: in your social groups, in physical activities, in school, and in everyday life.

💡 "We are like pillars — physical health, emotional health, and self-perception all need to be strong together."

😤

Sleep problems & constant tiredness

😔

What actually helps

Talk to someone you trust

Why this SDG matters

SDG 3 focuses on improving health and well-being for all people around the world. Health — including mental and emotional health — is essential for learning, productivity, happiness, and long-term success.
